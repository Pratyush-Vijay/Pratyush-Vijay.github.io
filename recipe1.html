Paneer butter masala

History

Paneer, a versatile and beloved ingredient in Indian cuisine, traces its roots back to the rich cultural tapestry of North India. Introduced to the region during the 16th century by Persian and Afghan rulers, paneer quickly embedded itself as a staple component of Indian culinary traditions.
Its journey from humble beginnings to culinary prominence reflects not only its nutritional value but also its profound cultural significance.

The recipe is rather simple, but it gives you a great end result worth making.

What you need:

Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 capsicum (bell pepper), diced (optional)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup cream
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

How To Make

Directions:

  1. Frying Paneer
    • Heat a tablespoon of ghee or butter in a pan over medium heat.
    • Add the paneer cubes and fry them until golden brown on all sides. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. Sauteing onions and spices
    • In the same pan, add another tablespoon of ghee or butter.
    • Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add minced garlic and grated ginger, and sauté until fragrant.
    • Add finely chopped onions and cook until they turn translucent.
    • Stir in the pureed tomatoes and diced capsicum (if using). Cook until the mixture thickens and the oil separates from the sides.
  3. Creating the Gravy:
    1. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for a few minutes to blend the flavours.
    2. Pour in the cream and stir until well combined.
    3. Season with salt according to taste.
  4. Adding paneer
    1. Gently add the fried paneer cubes to the gravy, ensuring they are coated evenly.
    2. Simmer the mixture for a few minutes to allow the flavours to meld together.
  5. Garnishing and serving
    1. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves for a burst of freshness and colour.
